How Compound Interest is Calculated (Formula)

Final amount = P × (1 + r/n)n×t + future value of contributions
P = principal, r = annual rate (decimal), n = compounds per year, t = years.
Monthly contributions are compounded using the monthly rate and added at year‑end (our calculator uses accurate series formula).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What’s the difference between simple and compound interest?

Simple interest is only on principal; compound interest is on principal + accumulated interest. Compound grows faster.

2. How does compounding frequency affect growth?

More frequent compounding (daily vs yearly) yields slightly higher returns because interest is added more often.

3. Can I use this for SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

Yes – set monthly contribution as your SIP amount. The calculator treats contributions as added at the end of each month.

4. Does the calculator include taxes or inflation?

It shows nominal growth. For real returns, use the “inflation” mode (demo) or adjust the rate manually (e.g., 7% return – 3% inflation = 4% real rate).

5. How accurate is the rule of 72?

Rule of 72 (72 ÷ rate) gives approximate doubling years. For 8% it says 9 years; exact is about 9.01 years – very close.

6. What if I want to make yearly contributions instead of monthly?

You can approximate by setting monthly contribution = yearly/12, or switch to the “SIP” mode (which allows annual).

7. Why is the final value different from a bank’s calculator?

Banks might use different assumptions (e.g., when contributions are added). Our calculator uses standard end‑of‑period convention.

8. Is this tool mobile responsive?

Yes – it adapts to any screen size, with collapsible advanced settings on small devices.
