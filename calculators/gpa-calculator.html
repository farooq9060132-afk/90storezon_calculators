What is a GPA Calculator?

A GPA Calculator helps students compute their Grade Point Average by entering course grades, credits, and grading scale. It supports multiple scales (4.0, 5.0, 10.0, percentage), weighted/unweighted modes, and even predicts what grades you need to reach a target GPA. Essential for academic planning.

How to Use the GPA Calculator (Step-by-Step Guide)

Step 1 Select your grading scale (4.0, 5.0, 10.0, or percent).
Step 2 Choose weighted or unweighted (advanced courses get extra points).
Step 3 Enter course names, credits, and letter grades.
Step 4 Add previous GPA & credits if you want cumulative CGPA.
Step 5 Instantly see your GPA, total points, and target requirements.

How GPA is Calculated (Formula)

GPA = (Σ grade points × credits) / Σ credits
Each letter grade corresponds to a point value (e.g., A=4.0). Weighted adds 0.5 for advanced courses.
Cumulative GPA combines previous GPA with current semester.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What’s the difference between weighted and unweighted?

Unweighted GPA uses a 4.0 scale for all courses. Weighted gives extra points (e.g., 4.5 or 5.0) for honors/AP classes.

2. Can I use this for percentage grades?

Yes, select “Percentage‑based” and enter percentages (e.g., 85). The calculator converts to grade points.

3. How do I calculate CGPA?

Enter your previous GPA and total credits in advanced settings. The CGPA updates automatically.

4. What is a “good” GPA?

In the US, 3.5+ is considered excellent (Dean’s list), 3.0‑3.5 good, below 2.5 needs improvement.

5. Can I save my semesters?

Yes – click “Save current” in advanced settings. History is stored in your browser.

6. How accurate is the target GPA calculation?

It uses the standard weighted average formula – exactly what schools use.

7. Is this calculator mobile‑friendly?

Fully responsive with sticky buttons and collapsible sections for easy use on phones.

8. What grading scales are supported?

4.0 (US), 5.0 (weighted), 10.0 (India), percentage, and custom – you can adjust values.
