How EMI is Calculated (Formula Explanation)

EMI is derived using the standard formula used by all banks:

EMI = P × r × (1+r)n / ((1+r)n – 1)

P = Principal loan amount · r = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100) · n = total months. Our calculator does the heavy lifting and also builds a complete amortization schedule.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Can I trust the EMI amount shown?

Yes, the calculator uses the same formula as banks and NBFCs. It matches lender calculations when parameters are identical.

2. What’s the difference between fixed and floating rate?

Fixed stays constant; floating changes with market. Our calculator works for fixed; use the “floating simulate” in advanced mode for estimates.

3. Does the calculator include processing fees?

Yes – you can enter a fee % and GST. The effective cost is shown in the advanced panel.

4. How does prepayment affect my loan?

Extra payment reduces principal faster, lowering total interest and shortening tenure. The “prepayment impact” line shows exact savings.

5. Can I use this for a car loan or education loan?

Absolutely. Any loan with monthly compounding works: car, education, mortgage, personal.

6. Why is my EMI different from what the bank quoted?

Banks might include insurance or other fees. Double‑check rate and tenure; our tool shows pure principal + interest.

7. What is an amortisation schedule?

It’s a table showing each payment’s split between interest and principal, plus the remaining balance after every month.

8. Is this calculator mobile‑friendly?

Yes, fully responsive on phones, tablets, and desktops – try it!
