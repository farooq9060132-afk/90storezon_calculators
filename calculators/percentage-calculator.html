What is a Percentage Calculator?

A percentage calculator is an online tool that instantly solves percentage problems: finding a percent of a number, calculating percentage increase or decrease, and determining what percent one number is of another. Instead of doing manual math or memorizing formulas, you just enter the numbers and get accurate results. It’s a must‑have for students, shoppers, business owners, and anyone dealing with proportions.

How to Use the Percentage Calculator (Step‑by‑Step Guide)

  1. Step 1: Enter the percentage value in the first field (e.g., 20 for 20%).
  2. Step 2: Enter the whole number (the “of” value) in the second field.
  3. Step 3: Click “Calculate % of number” — the result appears instantly in the third field and in the results panel.
  4. Step 4: Use the % increase button to add that percentage to the number, or % decrease to subtract it.
  5. Step 5: The panel also shows what percent one number is of another (e.g., “40 is 20% of 200”).
  6. Pro tip: Press Reset to clear all fields and start a fresh calculation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do I calculate percentage of a number?
Multiply the number by the percentage (as a decimal). Example: 20% of 200 = 200 × 0.2 = 40. Our calculator does it instantly.
What is the formula for percentage increase?
Increase = original number × (1 + percentage/100). For 20% increase on 200: 200 × 1.2 = 240.
Can I use this calculator for percentage decrease as well?
Absolutely! Click the “% decrease” button or manually subtract: original × (1 – percentage/100).
Does it handle decimals (like 8.5%)?
Yes, enter any decimal percentage (e.g., 8.5, 0.75). The calculator works with floating‑point numbers.
What does “40 is ? % of 400” mean?
It answers “what percent is A of B?”. Divide A by B and multiply by 100. Our tool shows that 40 is 10% of 400.
Is there a limit on the numbers I can enter?

Can I calculate reverse percentage (e.g., number given percentage)?
Yes, use the “X is Y% of what?” feature: the panel shows “40 is 20% of 200” — you can enter any two values to find the missing one.
