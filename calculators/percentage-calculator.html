Common Percentage Calculations

Here are the most frequently performed percentage calculations and the formulas behind them:

Basic Formula:

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Finding X% of a Number

To calculate a specific percentage of any number, multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 20% of 500 = (20 × 500) / 100 = 100. This is the most common calculation people need—from calculating tips to computing tax.

Percentage Increase

Use the percentage increase calculator formula: ((New Value − Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. If your salary went from $3,000 to $3,600, the increase is ((3600 − 3000) / 3000) × 100 = 20% increase.

Percentage Decrease

The percentage decrease calculator follows the same formula but yields a negative result. If a stock dropped from $80 to $64, the decrease is ((64 − 80) / 80) × 100 = −20%.

What Percent is X of Y?

To find what percentage one number is of another, divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. For example, 45 is what percent of 180? Answer: (45 / 180) × 100 = 25%.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How do I calculate the percentage of a number?

Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 20% of 150 = (20 × 150) / 100 = 30. In our tool, just select Basic mode, enter 20 and 150, and get the answer instantly.

2. What is the difference between percentage increase and percentage decrease?

Both use the same formula: ((New − Old) / Old) × 100. If the result is positive, it is an increase. If negative, it is a decrease. Our percentage increase calculator and percentage decrease calculator both use the Change mode.
