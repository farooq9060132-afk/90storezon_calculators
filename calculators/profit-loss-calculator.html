How Profit is Calculated (Formulas)

Profit = (Selling Price – Cost Price) × Quantity
Profit % (on CP) = (Profit / CP) × 100
Margin = (Profit / SP) × 100
Markup = (Profit / CP) × 100 (same as profit %).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What’s the difference between margin and markup?

Margin is profit % of selling price; markup is profit % of cost price. Example: CP $80, SP $100 → margin 20%, markup 25%.

2. How do I calculate loss percentage?

Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100. Our calculator shows it automatically when CP > SP.

3. Can I use this for service‑based pricing?

Yes – CP = your cost to deliver, SP = what you charge. Works perfectly.

4. What is break‑even point (BEP)?

BEP = Fixed cost / (Selling price – Variable cost per unit). The calculator shows it live in the advanced panel.

5. Does this calculator include taxes?

It shows pre‑tax profit. You can adjust SP to include tax or use the trading fee field as a tax proxy.

6. How accurate is the trading ROI?

ROI = (Net profit / Total cost) × 100, where total cost includes fees. It's exact.

7. Can I save my calculations?

Yes – use the “Save current” button. History is stored locally and can be exported as CSV.

8. Is this mobile friendly?

Absolutely – it adapts to any screen size with sticky buttons for easy use.
