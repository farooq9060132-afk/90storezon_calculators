What is a Simple Interest Calculator?

A Simple Interest Calculator computes interest earned or paid on a principal amount using the formula I = P × r × t. It’s the most straightforward way to see growth without compounding. But this tool goes further – it also lets you add periodic deposits that do compound, so you can model real‑world savings accounts or loans with extra contributions.

How to Use the Simple Interest Calculator (Step-by-Step Guide)

Step 1 Enter the principal amount (initial sum).
Step 2 Type the annual interest rate (%) and time (years).
Step 3 Choose how often interest compounds (yearly, quarterly, monthly, daily).
Step 4 Add a periodic deposit amount and frequency (monthly, yearly, one‑time).
Step 5 Watch all values update live – simple interest, total deposits, final balance.

How Simple Interest is Calculated (Formula)

Simple interest = Principal × annual rate × time (years)
Example: $10,000 at 5% for 3 years → 10,000 × 0.05 × 3 = $1,500 interest.

Deposits are compounded using the chosen frequency, so they earn interest on interest. The tool combines both: simple interest on the original principal + compounded future value of deposits.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What’s the difference between simple and compound interest?

Simple interest is only on the principal. Compound interest is on principal + previously earned interest. This tool shows both: simple on principal, compound on deposits.

2. Does the calculator include compound interest on deposits?

Yes – deposits earn interest at the chosen compounding frequency. The original principal earns simple interest only (clear separation).

3. Can I use this for a loan without deposits?

Absolutely. Just set deposit amount to 0 – you’ll get the pure simple interest amount and total repayment.

4. What does “one‑time deposit (end)” mean?

It adds a lump sum at the end of the period – it earns no interest, useful for final payments.

5. How accurate are the results?

Very accurate – uses standard financial formulas. For deposits, it applies the exact compound interest formula for the chosen frequency.

6. Can I change the currency?

The calculator is unit‑agnostic – just enter numbers in your preferred currency (the tool doesn’t add a currency symbol).

7. Why does compounding frequency matter for deposits?

More frequent compounding (daily vs yearly) increases the future value of deposits because interest is added more often.

8. Is this calculator mobile‑friendly?

Yes – it adapts perfectly to any screen size.
